reynolds number

¶ is basically a metric for quantifying/comparing viscosities. you compare the [viscosity] ˝ν˝ with the product of the flow’s characteristic velocity ˝V˝ and its characteristic length scale ˝L˝.

  Re = ÷{LV}{ν}
  • this is the reynolds number
  • it’s dimensionless
  • flows with ˝Re < 1˝ are dominated by viscosity
  • flows with ˝Re > 1˝ can be influenced by viscosity, especially when it acts near boundaries, BUT the viscous stresses are negligible over most of the flow’s volume
