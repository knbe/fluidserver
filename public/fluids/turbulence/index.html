RECAP: LAMINAR PIPE FLOW

¶ the flow velocity profile is parabolic (across the cylindrical cross section). you can achieve this form by balancing the stress across the surface.

¶ the rate of flow is proportional to the 4th power of pipe diameter:

  flowͺrateͺ(volumetricͺflux) ∝ d⁽4⁾

¶ reynolds number:

  Re₍pipe₎ = ÷1ν ⟨v⟩d

¶ experimentally pipe flow is only laminar up to a critical Re number, ˝∼10⁽3⁾-10⁽5⁾˝. the exact value depends on smoothness of the walls and the pipe entrance.

¶ turbulence: if you increase the Re number the critical number (e.g. by increasing the pressure gradient, thereby increasing ⟨v⟩, then the velocity field becomes irregular, spatially and temporally. you get turbulence.
