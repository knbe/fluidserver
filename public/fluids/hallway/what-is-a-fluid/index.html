¶ fluids are macroscopic. two reasons:

  1. the macroscopic continuum approximation is valid. ie observable quantities of the fluid (flow velocity, density, etc) VARY SMOOTHLY in space and time, at macroscopic scales. this is because the mean free paths of the molecules in a fluid are SMALL compared to macroscopic length scales.
