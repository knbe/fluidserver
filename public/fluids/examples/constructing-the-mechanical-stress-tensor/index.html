constructing the mechanical stress tensor.

¶ consider a small area element, d↗A. momentum flows through this area in the +ve direction. the MASS FLOW RATE thru the surface is

££ \begin{equation} ρ↗v ⦁ d↗A = dot(momentumͺdensity, vectorͺareaͺelement) \end{equation} ££

multiply by velocity to get the MOMENTUM FLOW RATE:

££ \begin{equation} ḍ₍t₎⬀p = (ρ⬀v)·(⬀v⦁d⬀A) \end{equation} ££

this is just the force ˝⬀F = ḍ⬀p˝ across the area element ˝d↗A˝. from the definition of the stress tensor, you can compute the force by inserting ˝d↗A˝ into the 2nd arg of a stress tensor,

££ \begin{equation} ↗F = d₍t₎↗p = T(ͺͺ,d↗A) \end{equation} ££

in index notation

££ \begin{equation} F⟦i⟧ = d₍t₎p⟦i⟧ = (p·v⟦i⟧)·v⟦j⟧·dA⟦j⟧ = T⟦ij⟧·dA⟦j⟧ \end{equation} ££

from the middle expression, the stress tensor is

££ \begin{equation} T⟦ij⟧ = ρ·v⟦i⟧·v⟦j⟧ \end{equation} ££

or

££ \begin{equation} T = ρ·↗v⊗↗v \end{equation} ££

¶ so the mechanical stress tensor is the MOMENTUM FLUX (the momentum flow rate).
