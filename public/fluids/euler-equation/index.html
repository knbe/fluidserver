euler equation

¶ take the momentum conservation law for a fluid,

££ \begin{equation} 𝝏₍t₎(ρ↗v) + ∇⦁T = ρ↗g \end{equation} ££

differentiate then invoke the mass conservation law,

££ \begin{equation} 𝝏₍t₎ρ + ∇⦁(ρ↗v) = 0 \end{equation} ££

you get the euler equation:

££ \begin{equation} Ɗ₍t₎↗v = 𝝏₍t₎↗v + (↗v⦁∇)↗v = -÷1ρ·∇p + ↗g \end{equation} ££

¶ the LHS is the advective derivative of ↗v, ie the acceleration of the fluid. from the RHS, you can see there are two terms that cause the acceleration: gravity, and the pressure gradient.

¶ in hydrostatics, ↗v = 0, and the euler equation reduces to the equation of hydrostatic equilibrium:

££ \begin{equation} ∇p = ρ↗g \end{equation} ££
